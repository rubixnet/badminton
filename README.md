# Badminton Tracker

Note: this readme is written with hel p of ai last minute! 

Stop losing scores in group chats.

Badminton Tracker is a fast, zero-clutter workspace designed for your weekend matches. Built to solve the problem of undocumented games and endless debates over who is actually the best player, this tool allows you to track scores, view win rates, and keep your rivalries recorded permanently. It is fully responsive, optimized for dark mode, and engineered to be used courtside between sets.

## Key Features
- Zero-Friction Scoring: A streamlined mobile interface that lets you log scores instantly, allowing you to focus on the game rather than fighting a clunky app.

- Momentum & Checkpoints: Go beyond the final score. Log mid-game checkpoints to capture the narrative of the match, automatically tagging major comebacks and sudden downfalls.

- Fault & Bonus Tracking: Add a layer of accountability. Use bonus and deduction points to record unforced errors, bad serves, or exceptional smashes to monitor player discipline.

- Detailed Player Profiles: Every player gets a dedicated dashboard featuring stat radars, average points per match, recent form history, and active win streaks.

- Head-to-Head Analytics: Settle debates with hard data. Track historical win/loss records against specific opponents to see who your toughest matchups really are.

- Deep Statistics: Access rich data visualizations, including win rate progression charts and a comprehensive yearly activity heatmap.

- Flexible Game Modes: Full support for both singles (1v1) and doubles (2v2) matchups, adapting to how your group actually plays.

- Multiplayer Clubs: Easily invite your friend group via a custom link, manage your roster, and let everyone view the shared leaderboards from their own devices.

The Story
This application was initially built as a personal tool. A lot of weekend matches were going undocumented, making it impossible to tell if anyone was actually improving or just getting lucky. Badminton Tracker was created to provide hard data for local courts, and it is now open for anyone who takes their friendly rivalries a bit too seriously.
